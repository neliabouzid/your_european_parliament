Incentivising defence-related investments in the EU budget to implement the ReArm Europe Plan
Subjects: Defence and arms industry; Structural funds, investment funds in general, programmes; Budget of the Union
Stage reached: Awaiting Council's 1st reading position
Reference: 2025/0103(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has introduced a legislative proposal aimed at enhancing defense-related investments within the EU budget, aligning with the ReArm Europe Plan. This initiative seeks to bolster the EU's defense capabilities by amending existing regulations to support increased investment in military resources and a coordinated response to emerging threats. A key component of the proposal is the expansion of the Strategic Technologies for Europe Platform (STEP) to include critical defense technologies such as air and missile defense, drones, cyber capabilities, and artificial intelligence. To achieve these objectives, the proposal suggests modifications to several EU programs, including the European Defence Fund (EDF), the Digital Europe Programme (DEP), and Horizon Europe. These changes are designed to pool funding, allow resource transfers from cohesion policy funds to the EDF, and support dual-use technologies that serve both civilian and military purposes. Additionally, the proposal aims to extend the Supporting Ammunition Production (ASAP) regulation and utilize unused funds from Horizon 2020 for new projects, with the goal of strengthening the EU's defense industry by ensuring long-term investment in new production capacities and enhancing the Union's technological and industrial base in defense. The proposal also emphasizes the importance of skills development in critical defense technologies through the European Social Fund Plus (ESF+) and advocates for a coordinated EU-level approach to ensure seamless mobility for military personnel and equipment. It suggests adapting the timeframe in the Financial Regulation to allow for more flexibility in decision-making.
