Trans-European energy infrastructure: guidelines
Subjects: Trans-European energy networks
Stage reached: Preparatory phase in Parliament
Reference: 2025/0399(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission's legislative proposal for "Trans-European energy infrastructure: guidelines" seeks to modernize and expand the EU's energy infrastructure, aligning with the bloc's renewable energy and decarbonization goals by 2040. The proposal emphasizes the development and integration of energy infrastructure through electrification and hydrogen systems, while also streamlining planning and permitting processes for cross-border energy projects to enhance network efficiency and resilience. This addresses challenges such as lengthy project completion times and permitting bottlenecks. To ensure timely development, the proposal introduces measures to bolster both physical and cyber security, responding to recent incidents and the growing interest of third-country actors in EU energy infrastructure. Projects of common and mutual interest are selected for inclusion in the Union list through a unified process based on transparent and objective criteria aligned with EU energy policy objectives. These projects must be part of the latest Union-wide ten-year network development plan, with sustainability as a key criterion. Specific criteria for these projects include contributions to sustainability through renewable energy integration, market integration, security of supply, network security, smart sector integration, and carbon dioxide emission reduction. For electricity transmission, distribution, and storage projects, contributions to sustainability, market integration, and security of supply are emphasized. Smart electricity grid projects must contribute to security of supply, market integration, network security, and smart sector integration. Carbon dioxide transport and storage projects should focus on sustainability and avoiding emissions. The proposal outlines several priority corridors and thematic areas for development, including offshore electricity grid developments.
