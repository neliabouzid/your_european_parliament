Amending certain agricultural products Regulations as regards certain market rules and sectoral support measures in the wine sector and for aromatised wine products
Subjects: Agricultural policy and economies; Marketing and trade of agricultural products and livestock; Wine, alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages
Stage reached: Awaiting Parliament's position in 1st reading
Reference: 2025/0071(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a legislative amendment aimed at revitalizing the European wine sector, which is grappling with challenges such as declining consumption, climate change impacts, and uncertain export markets. The proposal seeks to amend existing regulations to better manage wine production, enhance competitiveness, and explore new market opportunities. Key changes include allowing Member States to manage vine planting authorizations more effectively to prevent surplus production, introducing new labeling rules for wines with lower alcohol content, and providing flexibility for vineyard restructuring to prevent market imbalances. To support producers in adapting to climate change, the proposal allows Member States to increase financial assistance for investments in climate change mitigation and adaptation to up to 80% of eligible costs, excluding enterprises in financial difficulty. It also extends the validity of replanting authorizations to eight years, abolishes administrative penalties for unused replanting authorizations, and allows winegrowers to renounce unused authorizations granted before January 1, 2025, without penalty. The proposal gives Member States the ability to set regional limits on new planting authorizations in areas with excess supply, with flexibility to set these limits as low as 0% to adapt production to market demand. It also allows Member States to set conditions on the use of grape varieties and production methods to avoid yield increases and preserve traditional practices. A maximum threshold of hectares of planted vineyards is proposed, below which Member States are exempt from applying the planting authorizations scheme, addressing oversupply risks.
