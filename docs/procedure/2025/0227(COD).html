Commission's Proposal Summary

The "Global Europe" legislative proposal, introduced by the European Commission, seeks to overhaul the European Union's external action financing for the 2028-2034 Multiannual Financial Framework (MFF). This initiative is designed to address the challenges posed by a rapidly changing global environment, including geopolitical instability, economic challenges, and environmental concerns. The proposal's main goals are to promote EU values and strategic interests worldwide, address global challenges, and support sustainable development. To achieve these objectives, the proposal consolidates various financial instruments into a single main tool, governed by a unified performance regulation to oversee monitoring, reporting, and evaluation. This consolidation aims to simplify the EU's external action architecture, ensuring coherence by aligning geographical focus and consistency between internal and external programs. Additionally, the framework enhances flexibility through financial mechanisms that allow for easier budgetary adjustments, thereby increasing the impact of EU actions by streamlining processes and promoting European interests more effectively. A key component of the framework is the Investment Board, which includes representatives from the European Commission, the High Representative, all Member States, and the European Investment Bank (EIB). The European Parliament will have observer status, and other stakeholders may be given observer status where appropriate. The Board will be co-chaired by the Commission and the High Representative, ensuring a coordinated approach to investment decisions. The proposal emphasizes promoting human rights, democracy, and the rule of law, with specific attention to protecting the most vulnerable, including efforts against corruption and organized crime.
