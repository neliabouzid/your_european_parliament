Union support for asylum, migration and integration 2028–2034
Subjects: Free movement and integration of third-country nationals; Asylum, refugees, displaced persons; Asylum, Migration and Integration Fund (AMIF); Migration policy
Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0540(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a regulation to establish Union support for asylum, migration, and integration from 2028 to 2034, aiming to address migration as a collective challenge within the European Union. This proposal builds on the Pact on Migration and Asylum, seeking to create a comprehensive approach to managing asylum, migration, and border control through solidarity and shared responsibility among EU member states. It emphasizes the need for member states to develop efficient national systems, including significant financial investments in integrated case management systems and digitalization efforts, such as the use of artificial intelligence, to improve efficiency. The proposal also highlights the importance of supporting personnel needs, like case workers and legal advisers, to ensure the system functions effectively across the EU. To address evolving challenges in migration and asylum, the proposal introduces mechanisms for flexible and performance-oriented management of Union support, aligning with policy priorities and adapting to new developments. It complements other EU funds aimed at economic and social cohesion and seeks to support legal migration, early integration of third-country nationals, and innovative solutions for migration management. The proposal is part of a broader legal framework that includes regulations for the Schengen area, European integrated border management, and internal security, ensuring coherence and complementarity with existing EU policies. The proposal underscores the importance of consistency with other Union policies, such as border management, internal security, and the social inclusion and integration of third-country nationals. It also highlights the role of decentralized agencies like Frontex and Europol.
