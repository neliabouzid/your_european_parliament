Amendments to the Critical Raw Materials Act
Subjects: Industrial policy; Industrial competitiveness; Raw materials; Sustainable development; Financial and technical cooperation and assistance
Stage reached: Preparatory phase in Parliament
Reference: 2025/0385(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed amendments to the Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA) to bolster the EU's resilience and sustainability in securing essential raw materials vital for strategic technologies such as clean energy, digital transformation, and defense. This initiative is a response to geopolitical challenges, notably China's export restrictions on rare earth elements and other critical materials, which pose risks to the EU's supply chain stability. The amendments aim to establish a stable market within the EU, enhance industry resilience, and ensure consistent risk assessments across member states to prevent market fragmentation. Key objectives include expanding the circularity framework to increase the recovery and reuse of critical materials, such as rare earth permanent magnets. This involves broadening product labeling and information obligations and incorporating pre-consumer waste into recycling requirements. The proposal also introduces flexibility in the number of strategic project calls per year, reflecting the high volume of applications and the need for thorough assessments. These changes align with the broader RESourceEU Action Plan, supporting the EU's industrial and economic security goals. To address significant vulnerabilities to supply disruptions identified through risk assessments, large companies are required to take steps to mitigate these risks, such as diversifying their raw materials supply chains, considering secondary raw materials, or substituting strategic raw materials. These companies must present the results of their risk assessments to their board of directors or management board.
