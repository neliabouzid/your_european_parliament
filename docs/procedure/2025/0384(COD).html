Monitoring and controlling drug precursors
Subjects: Pharmaceutical products and industry; Export/import control, trade defence, trade barriers; Action to combat drugs and drug-trafficking
Stage reached: Preparatory phase in Parliament
Reference: 2025/0384(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a new regulation aimed at enhancing the monitoring and control of drug precursors within the EU. This initiative addresses the growing challenges posed by drug-related violence and the emergence of more potent drugs. The regulation focuses on substances that, while having legitimate industrial uses, can be diverted for illicit drug production. A key component of the proposal is the creation of a Repository managed by the European Union Drugs Agency (EUDA). This Repository will gather information on both scheduled and non-scheduled drug precursors, aiding operators in identifying suspicious transactions and assisting authorities in deciding whether to regulate new substances. The proposal underscores the importance of digitalization in reducing costs and administrative burdens for the chemical industry, a crucial sector in the EU. It requires operators involved in the market, import, export, and intermediary activities with scheduled drug precursors to maintain detailed commercial documents for each transaction. Additionally, the proposal includes provisions for training national authorities and operators, promoting cooperation between member states, and enhancing the powers of customs and competent authorities to monitor and control precursor diversion. Operators must request updates to their licenses if they plan to exceed estimated quantities, handle new substances, undertake additional activities, or change business premises. Competent authorities may update licenses based on documentary checks, and decisions to refuse, revoke, or suspend licenses will be communicated electronically and are subject to appeal under national law.
