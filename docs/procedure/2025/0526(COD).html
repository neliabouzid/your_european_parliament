Dates of application and transitional provisions for chemical products
Subjects: Chemical industry, fertilizers, plastics
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0526(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a regulation aimed at modernizing and simplifying the legislative framework governing chemical products within the EU. This initiative is part of a broader effort to reduce regulatory burdens and streamline processes for businesses, particularly in the chemical industry, which is vital for the EU's economy and innovation. The proposal seeks to amend existing regulations related to the classification, labeling, and packaging of chemical substances, mixtures, cosmetic products, and fertilizing products. Key aspects of the proposal include making labeling requirements for hazardous chemicals more flexible, addressing costly issues such as mandatory font sizes and line spacing. It also aims to simplify labeling for smaller packages and fuel pumps, and ease obligations related to advertisements and distance sales of chemical products, recognizing that existing regulations already cover professional supply chains effectively. Additionally, the proposal suggests removing the fixed six-month deadline for updating labels, allowing updates to occur without undue delay, and expanding the use of digital labeling to provide more information digitally. To ensure a smooth transition, the proposal outlines specific application dates for the new requirements, aiming to provide legal certainty and consistency across different regulations. It also addresses specific formatting requirements and deadlines for relabeling in case of classification changes, as well as information requirements for advertisements and distance sales offers. The proposal seeks to balance the clarity of label information for consumers with the need to reduce market barriers and administrative burdens for the industry.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to amend existing rules on the classification, labeling, and packaging of chemical products, aiming to simplify regulations and reduce administrative burdens on businesses. This amendment defers the implementation of certain labeling requirements, particularly addressing the impracticality and high costs associated with labeling fuels at filling stations. It extends the transitional period for businesses to voluntarily apply new rules, allowing more time for preparation. The regulation maintains certain provisions to protect human health and the environment while streamlining processes to enhance the competitiveness of European businesses. Specific derogations allow substances and mixtures to be classified, labeled, and packaged under previous regulations until specified dates, providing flexibility during the transition. The regulation will enter into force twenty days after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union and will be directly applicable in all Member States.
