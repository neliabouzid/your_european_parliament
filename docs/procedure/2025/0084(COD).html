Amending ERDF, Cohesion Fund and Just Transition Fund as regards specific measures to address strategic challenges in the context of the mid-term review
Subjects: Structural funds, investment funds in general, programmes; Cohesion policy, Cohesion Fund (CF); European Regional Development Fund (ERDF)
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0084(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed amendments to the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), Cohesion Fund, and Just Transition Fund as part of a mid-term review aimed at addressing strategic challenges and priorities. This proposal seeks to realign resources to enhance the EU's defense capabilities, competitiveness, and strategic autonomy, while also focusing on emerging priorities such as clean industrial deals and climate resilience. Recognizing that the current regulatory framework is outdated due to recent geopolitical and economic shifts, the Commission suggests adjustments to increase flexibility and accelerate investments in critical areas like technological leadership, energy independence, and climate resilience. The proposal addresses challenges faced by energy-intensive industries, such as higher energy costs and increased international competition, which threaten Europe's decarbonization and resilience goals. To counter these challenges, the cohesion funds are encouraged to support investments aligned with climate objectives, accelerating efforts to ensure decarbonization drives growth for European industries. Member States are urged to invest in projects contributing to the climate and energy transition, with support for projects within the Strategic Energy Technology Plan (STEP) being made possible in all regions, including more developed ones, as allowed by state aid rules. To provide a flexible financial support framework, the Commission proposes two new specific objectives within the ERDF. The first allows Member States to reprogram funds under their 2021-2027 programs to enhance productive capacities in the defense sector, without geographic or enterprise size restrictions. The second objective focuses on building resilient defense or dual-use infrastructure to improve military mobility in the EU.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation amending the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), Cohesion Fund, and Just Transition Fund to address strategic challenges identified during a mid-term review. This amendment aims to enhance the EU's strategic autonomy, resilience, and competitiveness amid geopolitical uncertainties and transitions towards green, social, and technological advancements. Key initiatives include mobilizing cohesion policy funding to support investments in defense capabilities and civil security, with a focus on military mobility, cybersecurity, and regional development, particularly in the Union’s Eastern border regions. The regulation prioritizes projects that enhance employment, skills development, and industrial diversification, supporting small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in dual-use technologies, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence. Additionally, it encourages investments in transport networks to meet military requirements while benefiting civilian mobility and economic connectivity. The regulation extends financing options, including one-off pre-financing and higher Union co-financing rates, to facilitate these investments. It also supports investments aligned with the Strategic Technologies for Europe Platform (STEP), promoting energy interconnectors, transmission, distribution, and storage infrastructure, and enhancing the deployment of charging infrastructure. Furthermore, the regulation encourages investments in important projects of common European interest (IPCEIs) to support large-scale, cross-border projects essential for the EU's economic growth and competitiveness. To address the challenge of affordable and sustainable housing, new specific objectives have been created to incentivize investments in the construction and renovation of housing stock.
