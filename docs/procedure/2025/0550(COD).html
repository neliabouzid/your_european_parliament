'AgoraEU' programme 2028–2034
Subjects: Citizen's rights; Child protection, children's rights; Women condition and rights; Common cultural area, cultural diversity
Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0550(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The proposed 'AgoraEU' programme, spanning from 2028 to 2034, is a strategic initiative by the European Commission aimed at revitalizing Europe's cultural, media, and creative sectors. This comprehensive programme seeks to establish a supportive framework that emphasizes inclusive governance, the promotion of fundamental rights, and the celebration of cultural diversity. Recognizing the pivotal role of media and cultural expressions in shaping public opinion, fostering societal resilience, and driving economic growth, the programme addresses significant challenges such as threats to media pluralism, declining revenues for news media, and the dominance of non-EU players in the audiovisual and video game markets. It also tackles the fragmentation of cultural and creative sectors along national lines, which hinders transnational collaboration and limits market access. To support these objectives, the programme will introduce new budget lines within the multiannual financial framework, specifically targeting areas such as Creative Europe - Culture, MEDIA+, and CERV+ (Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values). These budget lines will see commitments increasing annually from 2028 to 2034, with a total of 8.582 billion EUR allocated for commitments over the seven-year period. The programme also includes support expenditure for AgoraEU, although specific payment figures are yet to be determined. In addition to operational appropriations, the programme outlines administrative expenditures, including human resources and other administrative costs, totaling 270.478 million EUR over the same period.
