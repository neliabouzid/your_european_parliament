Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism: simplification and strengthening
Subjects: Customs union, tax and duty-free, Community transit; Atmospheric pollution, motor vehicle pollution; Climate policy, climate change, ozone layer; Environmental taxation; Common commercial policy in general
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0039(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a regulation to refine and enhance the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), aiming to align carbon pricing for imported goods with those produced within the EU. This initiative seeks to reduce the administrative burden on businesses, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises, by simplifying complex reporting requirements and data collection processes. The proposal maintains CBAM's environmental objectives, ensuring it continues to effectively address climate change by aligning carbon pricing for imports with internal EU standards. It also enhances the Commission's ability to monitor and supervise CBAM, improving data processing and information exchange with national authorities to maximize the utility of reported data and better detect risks. To ease compliance, the proposal introduces a threshold to exempt certain importers from CBAM obligations, targeting those within the CBAM scope with changes to reporting requirements. This threshold considers all imported emissions over a period, applying a mass-based threshold reflecting the average emissions intensity of imported goods in specific sectors cumulatively during a calendar year. Importers established in a Member State must apply for the status of authorized CBAM declarant before importing goods into the EU. The proposal also includes specific amendments, such as refining the classification of certain goods and adding new tables for greenhouse gas emissions related to electricity. These changes are part of a broader initiative to create a more efficient regulatory environment that supports European competitiveness and resilience. The overarching objective of the CBAM is to address climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions both within the EU and globally.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to amend the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), aiming to simplify and strengthen its implementation. The CBAM ensures that imported goods into the EU face similar carbon costs as those produced within the EU, addressing carbon emissions associated with these imports. A key change is the introduction of a "single mass-based threshold" of 50 tonnes, exempting smaller importers of specific goods—such as iron and steel, aluminium, fertilisers, and cement—from CBAM obligations if their imports do not exceed this limit. This exemption is designed to reduce administrative burdens while maintaining the environmental goals of the CBAM, ensuring that at least 99% of emissions from imported goods remain covered. However, this exemption does not apply to electricity and hydrogen imports due to their unique trade and emission characteristics. The regulation mandates that the Commission assesses annually whether significant changes in emission intensities or trade patterns necessitate adjustments to this threshold, ensuring that at least 99% of emissions remain within the CBAM's scope. Importers exceeding the threshold must obtain authorization as a CBAM declarant and submit declarations for the emissions embedded in their goods. Indirect customs representatives, who act on behalf of importers, must also comply with these obligations and face penalties for non-compliance, except when they have not agreed to act as authorized declarants. To prevent import disruptions, importers expecting to exceed the threshold can apply for authorization, allowing them to continue importing pending approval.
