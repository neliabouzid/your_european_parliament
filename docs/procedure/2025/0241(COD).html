Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0241(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a new regulation for the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) for the period 2028–2034, aiming to modernize and enhance the policy's effectiveness in addressing the evolving challenges faced by Europe's agricultural sector. The proposal seeks to provide targeted income support to farmers, ensuring their competitiveness while contributing to food security and environmental preservation. It emphasizes directing support to farmers who actively contribute to these goals and encourages them to diversify their income sources. To make farming more attractive, particularly to younger generations, the proposal promotes skills development, better access to capital, and improved working conditions. It introduces a performance-based and market-oriented approach, reducing bureaucratic burdens and rewarding environmentally friendly practices. The proposal also stresses the need for a stable and predictable policy framework to help farmers tackle environmental and climate challenges, with Member States taking greater responsibility for meeting policy objectives through incentives rather than mandatory conditions. The proposal includes measures to differentiate payments, targeting support at farmers most in need, such as young and new farmers, women, family or small farmers, and those in areas with natural or other specific constraints. It introduces a degressive payment system, where higher income farmers receive reduced support, and sets a cap on the total amount of area-based income support per farmer. Additionally, the proposal ensures that support is primarily directed towards farmers actively contributing to food security and allows for the inclusion of small farmers and those with natural constraints in the eligible hectare calculations.
