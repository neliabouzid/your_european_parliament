European Social Fund (ESF+): specific measures to address strategic challenges
Subjects: European Social Fund (ESF), Fund for European Aid to the Most Deprived (FEAD)
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0085(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed amendments to the European Social Fund Plus (ESF+) regulation to better align it with the EU's strategic priorities, addressing challenges such as geopolitical shifts, the green transition, and technological advancements. This proposal, submitted to the European Parliament, aims to enhance the EU's economic resilience and strategic autonomy by allowing Member States to redirect resources towards new priorities, including defense capabilities, clean industrial deals, and skills development to tackle labor shortages. The mid-term review of the cohesion policy offers an opportunity to reassess and adjust the implementation of these funds to better respond to current challenges, emphasizing the need for greater flexibility and incentives to accelerate the deployment of resources. The proposal highlights the importance of equipping Europe's workforce with the necessary skills to support the growing defense industry and the transition to a low-carbon economy. This includes skills in clean technologies, digitalization, and entrepreneurship. In response to geopolitical instability, the EU is considering a significant increase in support for defense capabilities, including a proposed EUR 800 billion response plan, REARM Europe, to bolster European defense capacities. The ESF+ will facilitate the development of skills in the defense industry by providing additional flexibilities, such as increased pre-financing and co-financing. For Eastern border regions affected by the Russian aggression against Ukraine, the proposal suggests a one-off 9.5% pre-financing of program allocations in 2026 with 100% Union financing.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation amending the European Social Fund Plus (ESF+), effective from September 18, 2025, to enhance its flexibility in addressing strategic challenges faced by the EU and its Member States. This amendment allows for more adaptable use of ESF+ resources to tackle geopolitical and economic challenges while maintaining core objectives like social inclusion, cohesion, and labor market activation. The regulation emphasizes balancing emergency responses with long-term social goals and encourages investment in social objectives alongside defense and cybersecurity. It supports worker and enterprise adaptation to decarbonization efforts, focusing on skill development and job creation, particularly in less developed regions and rural areas. The amendment also introduces measures to ensure effective use of funds by not allowing program amendments for objectives that fail to meet certain conditions, thereby incentivizing compliance with EU objectives. Additionally, it provides for increased pre-financing for investments aligned with the Strategic Technologies for Europe Platform (STEP) and offers additional financial support to regions heavily impacted by Russia's aggression against Ukraine. This includes higher pre-financing percentages and co-financing rates for affected regions, along with adjusted deadlines for financial management and reporting to facilitate resource reallocation. The regulation also underscores the importance of skills development and training, particularly for young talent and entrepreneurs, to align with labor market needs and support job creation in rural and less developed areas. When amending programs, Member States are required to include obligations for beneficiaries to respect working and employment conditions, with the involvement of social partners.
