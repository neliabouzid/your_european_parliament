Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation amending capital requirements for banks, specifically addressing securities financing transactions (SFTs) and their impact on the net stable funding ratio (NSFR). The NSFR is a measure designed to ensure that banks maintain a stable funding base relative to their assets and activities, thereby reducing their reliance on short-term funding. Initially, there was a proposal to increase stable funding factors for certain short-term transactions to encourage more conservative funding practices. However, the European Banking Authority determined that such changes would have minimal impact on banks' NSFR levels but could negatively affect the liquidity of sovereign debt markets and create funding challenges for member states. As a result, the regulation maintains the current stable funding factors for these transactions indefinitely. To ensure legal certainty and minimize administrative burdens, the regulation will take effect on June 29, 2025. The European Banking Authority is tasked with monitoring the stable funding required for SFTs and unsecured transactions with financial customers with a residual maturity of less than six months. They will report to the Commission by January 31, 2029, and every five years thereafter, assessing the appropriateness of these requirements in light of international developments and the regulatory treatment of similar transactions in other jurisdictions. This includes evaluating the impact of stable funding factors on the price structure of transactions and the market liquidity of assets received as collateral, particularly sovereign and corporate bonds.
