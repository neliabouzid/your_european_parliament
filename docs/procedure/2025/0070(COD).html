Additional flexibility as regards the calculation of manufacturers’ compliance with CO2 emission performance standards for new passenger cars and new light commercial vehicles for the calendar years 2025 to 2027
Subjects: Standardisation, EC/EU standards and trade mark, certification, compliance; Road transport: passengers and freight; Motor industry, cycle and motorcycle, commercial and agricultural vehicles; Atmospheric pollution, motor vehicle pollution
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0070(COD)

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation granting car manufacturers additional flexibility in meeting CO2 emission performance standards for new passenger cars and light commercial vehicles from 2025 to 2027. This regulation allows manufacturers to calculate their compliance with emission targets over this three-year period as an average, rather than annually, offering a one-off flexibility while maintaining overall emission reduction goals. Manufacturers must ensure that their average CO2 emissions do not exceed their calculated targets over these years. Additionally, the regulation permits pooling agreements, which allow manufacturers to combine their emissions data to meet targets, to be formed for the years 2025 or 2026 until the end of 2027. Excess emissions beyond the set targets during this period will result in penalties. This measure aims to balance the need for flexibility with the commitment to reducing emissions, addressing stakeholder requests while ensuring that the broader environmental objectives are met. The regulation will become effective on the twentieth day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union and will be directly applicable in all EU member states.
