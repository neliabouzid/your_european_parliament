Economic and budgetary surveillance of Member States in the euro area experiencing or threatened with serious difficulties with respect to their financial stability
Subjects: Structural funds, investment funds in general, programmes
Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0312(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a revision to the regulation overseeing economic and budgetary surveillance of euro area Member States facing or at risk of financial difficulties. This update aims to align the 2013 framework with recent changes in the EU's economic governance and institutional developments. Originally, the regulation was designed to monitor and support countries experiencing financial instability, either through enhanced surveillance or by integrating them into macroeconomic adjustment programs linked to financial assistance from the European Stability Mechanism (ESM). It also included provisions for ongoing surveillance after financial assistance was provided, until a significant portion of the aid was repaid. Since its inception, the EU's economic governance framework has seen significant reforms, introducing new concepts like national medium-term fiscal-structural plans. Additionally, the establishment of the Single Supervisory Mechanism (SSM) has changed the supervision landscape for financial entities, and the European Financial Stability Facility (EFSF) has stopped engaging in new financing programs. The proposed amendments aim to ensure consistency between the existing regulation and the updated governance framework, addressing issues identified in past applications, such as the deterrent effect of enhanced surveillance on countries seeking financial assistance and the lack of clear objectives in post-programme surveillance. Key changes in the proposal include broadening the scope of financial assistance sources to include not only other Member States and mechanisms like the European Financial Stabilisation Mechanism (EFSM) and ESM but also third countries and other relevant entities.
