Certain corporate sustainability reporting and due diligence requirements: application dates
Subjects: Company law; Sustainable development
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0044(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a directive to amend existing legislation on corporate sustainability reporting and due diligence, focusing on adjusting the application dates for these regulations. This initiative aims to simplify regulatory frameworks and reduce administrative burdens, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). The proposal is part of a broader effort to enhance competitiveness amid economic and geopolitical challenges, such as rising energy prices and trade tensions. Central to this proposal are the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) and the Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD). The CSRD, effective since January 2023, requires companies to disclose their sustainability impacts, aiding investors and stakeholders in understanding risks related to climate change and other sustainability issues. The CSDDD, adopted in June 2024, mandates companies to address adverse human rights and environmental impacts within their operations and supply chains. To help EU companies maintain competitiveness and adapt to the global landscape, the proposal seeks to delay the implementation of certain CSRD and CSDDD provisions. This phased implementation requires large public interest entities to start reporting in 2025, with other companies following in subsequent years. Additionally, the proposal involves developing European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) to guide these requirements. The directive aims to postpone the application date for the first group of companies under the CSDDD by one year, allowing more time for preparation and the opportunity to consider guidelines on fulfilling due diligence obligations. Furthermore, the deadline for member states to transpose the CSDDD is extended by one year.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a directive aimed at revising corporate sustainability reporting and due diligence requirements across the EU. This directive, effective from April 14, 2025, simplifies obligations for businesses by adjusting implementation dates and reducing administrative burdens. Large companies and public-interest entities will begin reporting in 2027 for financial years starting in 2026, while smaller companies will report in 2028 for financial years beginning in 2027. New due diligence requirements are postponed by one year, allowing companies more time to adapt. The directive specifies that certain large companies, both EU-based and those formed in third countries, must comply with specific measures from July 26, 2028, with some exceptions applying from January 1, 2029. Other companies will follow from July 26, 2029, with certain measures starting January 1, 2030. Member States are required to implement the necessary laws by December 31, 2025, and inform the Commission. The directive emphasizes the EU's commitment to achieving its objectives effectively, adhering to principles of subsidiarity and proportionality, and will enter into force the day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.
