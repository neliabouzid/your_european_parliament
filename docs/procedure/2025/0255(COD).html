Justice programme 2028-2034
Subjects: Judicial cooperation in civil and commercial matters; Judicial cooperation in criminal matters
Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0255(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission's proposed Justice Programme for 2028-2034 seeks to enhance the area of freedom, security, and justice within the EU by focusing on strengthening the independence, efficiency, and adherence to fundamental rights of justice systems across member states. The initiative addresses challenges such as gaps in legal protection and uneven progress in digitalization, emphasizing judicial independence and universal access to justice. A significant aspect of the proposal is the use of EU funding to bolster national justice systems and improve the capabilities of legal practitioners. The programme highlights the importance of digital tools and artificial intelligence in modernizing justice systems, making them more accessible, transparent, and efficient. This modernization is expected to enhance legal certainty and cross-border cooperation, with digital systems providing resilience during crises and reinforcing public trust. Feedback from stakeholders, including EU citizens and organizations, informed the proposal, focusing on upholding the rule of law and protecting children's rights. To address transnational challenges, the programme considers the specific needs of different policy areas and target groups. It will publish annual calls for proposals, supporting activities like conferences, expert meetings, and training material development. Designed to be flexible, the programme will adapt to emerging priorities over its duration. A critical aspect of the proposal is the management and control system, which aims to maintain the expected levels of risk of error below the materiality threshold of 2%. To prevent fraud and irregularities, the European Commission's Directorate-General for Justice will continue to oversee the programme.
