Pericles V exchange, assistance and training programme for the protection of the euro against counterfeiting 2028–2034
Subjects: Single currency, euro, euro area; Action against counterfeiting
Stage reached: Awaiting committee decision
Reference: 2025/0258(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed the "Pericles V" programme, a legislative initiative designed to enhance the protection of the euro against counterfeiting from 2028 to 2034. Building on the success of its predecessor, "Pericles IV," the programme aims to maintain the euro's integrity by addressing the threat of high-quality counterfeit currency. It focuses on training and capacity-building for authorities, fostering cooperation among EU member states, and coordinating with other security initiatives to bolster anti-counterfeiting efforts. A key feature of the proposal is its extension to non-euro member states through a parallel Council Regulation, ensuring a unified approach across the EU. The programme includes actions such as the exchange and dissemination of information, technical and operational assistance, and the purchase of equipment for specialized anti-counterfeiting authorities in third countries. These actions are to be implemented through grants, with the stipulation that equipment purchases are not the sole component of the grant agreement. Eligible actions under the programme require participation from trainers and/or trainees from at least two member states or third countries, and from at least two professional groups. Funding is available to competent national authorities, and in cases where third countries participate, their appointed authorities are also eligible. The programme will be implemented through work programmes, with specific eligibility criteria potentially outlined further in these programmes. To ensure the integrity and proper implementation of the programme, all files will be verified by at least four agents before acceptance by the Authorising Officer.
