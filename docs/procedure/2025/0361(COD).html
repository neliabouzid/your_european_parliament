Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed amendments to the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) to enhance transparency and effectiveness within the EU's sustainable finance framework. Initially introduced in 2021, the SFDR aims to improve how financial products incorporate environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors, helping investors make informed decisions and combat misleading sustainability claims, often termed "greenwashing." Despite its success in increasing ESG-characteristic financial products, the regulation has faced criticism for its complexity and the challenges financial institutions encounter in accessing reliable ESG data. To address these issues, the Commission's proposal seeks to simplify the SFDR, reduce administrative burdens, and improve enforcement. The goal is to maintain the integrity of the EU's single market for sustainable finance by focusing on requirements that prevent greenwashing and enhance investors' ability to compare sustainability-linked financial products. This effort aligns with the European Green Deal's objectives and aims to simplify EU regulations to enhance competitiveness and efficiency. The proposal also aims to boost the competitiveness of Europe’s financial sector by making business conditions easier and deepening the single market for sustainability-linked financial products, thereby helping to allocate capital for Europe’s sustainable prosperity. The proposal includes simplifying and reducing sustainability-related administrative and disclosure requirements for financial market participants and advisers, while enhancing the framework's coherence for operational needs. It seeks to improve end-investors' understanding and comparison of sustainability-linked financial products.
