Habitats Directive: protection status of the wolf (Canis lupus)
Subjects: Animal protection; Protection of natural resources: fauna, flora, nature, wildlife, countryside; biodiversity
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0058(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed an amendment to the Habitats Directive to align with a recent decision by the Standing Committee of the Bern Convention, which oversees wildlife conservation in Europe. This decision has reclassified the protection status of the wolf (Canis lupus) from "strictly protected" to "protected." As a result, the EU must update its legislation, moving the wolf from Annex IV, which lists species requiring strict protection, to Annex V, where species are protected but can be subject to certain conditions. This change means that while the wolf remains protected, the strict prohibitions against its capture, killing, and disturbance will be relaxed. Instead, the wolf will be subject to regulations ensuring its conservation status remains favorable, allowing for some controlled activities under specific conditions. The proposal reflects the EU's commitment to fulfilling its international obligations under the Bern Convention and involves consultation with national parliaments and various EU bodies. Member States are allowed to maintain or introduce more stringent protective measures for the wolf if they are compatible with EU treaties. The directive will come into effect 20 days after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union, with Member States required to implement necessary laws and regulations within 18 months. This legislative change aims to contribute to biodiversity conservation by adjusting the legal framework governing the protection of the wolf in Europe. Regarding the budget, the proposal does not anticipate any financial impact. There is no provision for co-financing by third parties, and the proposal has no financial impact on revenue.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council of the European Union have adopted a directive amending the protection status of the wolf (Canis lupus) under European Union law. This change reflects a decision made by the Standing Committee of the Bern Convention, which moved the wolf from a category of strictly protected species to a category of protected species. As a result, the wolf is no longer listed under Annex IV of the EU's Habitats Directive, which previously provided it with stricter protection. However, the directive allows individual EU member states to maintain or introduce more stringent protective measures for wolves if they choose. Member states are required to implement the necessary laws and regulations to align with this directive by January 15, 2027. This directive aims to harmonize the EU's legal framework with its international obligations under the Bern Convention, while still allowing flexibility for member states to decide on their level of protection for wolves.
