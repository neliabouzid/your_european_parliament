Common rules for imports: suspension of certain parts of Regulation (EU) 2015/478 as regards imports of Ukrainian products into the European Union
Subjects: Export/import control, trade defence, trade barriers; Union Customs Code, tariffs, preferential arrangements, rules of origin
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0056(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a regulation to temporarily suspend certain parts of an existing EU regulation governing the import of Ukrainian products, particularly steel, to support Ukraine amid economic challenges caused by Russia's invasion. This proposal is part of the EU's broader strategy to assist Ukraine through financial aid, military support, and sanctions against Russia, aligning with the EU's commitment to its foundational principles. By reducing restrictions on imports from Ukraine, the regulation aims to provide economic relief and enhance Ukraine's market access within the EU, benefiting both parties without increasing the regulatory burden on companies. The suspension is designed to respect fundamental rights and principles, consistent with the EU-Ukraine Association Agreement, which emphasizes democratic values, human rights, and the rule of law. The regulation is set to enter into force on June 6, 2025, coinciding with the expiry of a related regulation, and will initially apply for three years, with the possibility of renewal unless opposed by the European Parliament or the Council three months before its expiry. The Commission will have the authority to suspend the regulation for specific products if their import levels significantly harm EU industries producing similar products. This decision will follow a committee procedure involving the Committee on Safeguards. Additionally, the proposal includes anti-fraud measures, requiring Ukraine to comply with conditions under the Association Agreement, including rules of origin and administrative cooperation. The proposal is expected to have no financial implications on the EU budget.
