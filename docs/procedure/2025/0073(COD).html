European Globalisation Adjustment Fund for Displaced Workers (EGF): support to workers affected by imminent job displacement in enterprises undergoing restructuring
Subjects: Industrial restructuring, job losses, redundancies, relocations, Globalisation Adjustment Fund (EGF)
Stage reached: Awaiting Parliament's position in 1st reading
Reference: 2025/0073(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed an amendment to the European Globalisation Adjustment Fund for Displaced Workers (EGF) to extend its support to workers facing imminent job displacement due to enterprise restructuring. Traditionally, the EGF has assisted workers who have already lost their jobs, but this proposal aims to provide early support to those at risk of redundancy. This proactive approach is designed to mitigate the impact of job losses by offering personalized support packages, including education and retraining, to help affected workers transition to new roles or jobs. The proposal is driven by the need to address economic disruptions, such as those caused by structural shifts in the economy and global challenges, which have led to a rise in job cuts. While the EU has seen significant employment growth in recent years, recent data indicates a reversal, with job cuts surpassing job creations. The EGF, as an emergency response tool, is intended to fill the gap left by longer-term support mechanisms like the European Social Fund Plus, which cannot be quickly reprogrammed to address immediate needs. To facilitate this support, the proposal includes a streamlined procedure for mobilizing the EGF's full annual budget at the start of each year, allowing for rapid deployment of resources to affected workers. This approach underscores the EGF's role as a reactive instrument to support workers during economic transitions, ensuring they have access to necessary skills and opportunities to maintain employment and contribute to the EU's economic competitiveness.
