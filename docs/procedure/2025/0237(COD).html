Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed a series of amendments to the Common Market Organisation (CMO) Regulation, aiming to bolster the agricultural and food sectors within the EU. These changes are designed to enhance the strategic role of farming and food production, ensuring food security for 450 million Europeans, supporting rural economies, and promoting environmental sustainability. The proposal addresses challenges such as global competition, import dependencies, and geopolitical uncertainties that impact EU farmers. Key elements of the proposal include enhancing the EU school scheme for fruit, vegetables, and milk, and introducing sectoral interventions to support specific agricultural sectors like protein crops and hemp. A new protein crop sector is proposed to reduce reliance on imports and promote sustainable livestock farming, crucial for the EU's agricultural competitiveness and cohesion. The proposal also emphasizes preparedness in the agricultural sector to ensure supply availability during emergencies and crises, advocating for better coordination among EU Member States and the Commission. To address supply chain vulnerabilities, the proposal introduces legal provisions requiring Member States to adopt baseline preparedness measures, including the establishment of national and/or regional preparedness and response plans for agricultural products. This includes regular sharing of information on stocks, designation of competent authorities, and participation in EU-level stress testing exercises. Enhanced obligations during crises or high-risk situations, such as mandatory reporting, are also proposed. The Union Stockpiling strategy aims to combine centralized reserves with contributions from Member States, supported by public-private partnerships to ensure efficiency, scalability, and cost-effectiveness.
