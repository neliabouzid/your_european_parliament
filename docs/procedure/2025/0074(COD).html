Extension of the timeframe for the establishment of the Eurojust case management system
Subjects: Action to combat crime; Judicial cooperation in criminal matters; Agencies and bodies of the EU
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0074(COD)

Commission's Proposal Summary

The European Commission has proposed an extension for Eurojust, the EU agency tasked with coordinating cross-border criminal investigations, to transition to a new case management system (CMS). This system is crucial for securely exchanging data among national authorities, but the current CMS is outdated and requires an upgrade to improve functionality and security. Originally, the transition was scheduled for completion by December 2025, but significant delays have occurred due to challenges with the external contractor and the complex data migration process. To address these issues, the proposal seeks to legally extend the use of the old CMS beyond the initial deadline, ensuring Eurojust can continue its operations without legal repercussions for data handling. This extension is vital to prevent potential legal challenges that could affect the admissibility of evidence in court cases. The proposal aligns with broader EU strategies for digitalization and data protection, aiming to enhance the interoperability of justice systems across the EU. By allowing more time for the transition, the proposal aims to ensure a smooth and secure upgrade to the CMS, supporting Eurojust's mission to combat serious cross-border crime effectively. The proposed amendment extends the deadline for maintaining the old CMS and establishing the new CMS from December 1, 2025, to December 1, 2027. This change provides Eurojust with two additional years to ensure a successful transition. The migration from the old to the new CMS requires significant manual intervention, as each national desk of Eurojust stores information differently, necessitating several months for data migration.

Final Act Summary

The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to extend the deadline for establishing Eurojust's new case management system, which is designed to securely store and exchange operational personal data related to criminal justice cooperation across the EU. Originally set for December 1, 2025, the deadline has been extended to December 1, 2027, due to the complexity of the system's migration and external factors. This extension allows Eurojust to continue using its existing system of temporary work files and an index until the new system is fully operational and data migration is complete. The new system aims to enhance Eurojust's capabilities in managing cross-border judicial proceedings, particularly in terrorism cases, by integrating functionalities from the European Judicial Counter-Terrorism Register and improving data interoperability. The regulation ensures that Eurojust can maintain data accuracy and operationality during this transition period. Notably, Ireland and Denmark are not participating in or bound by this regulation due to their specific positions within the EU's legal framework.
