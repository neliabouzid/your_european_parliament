Common rules for imports: suspension of certain parts of Regulation (EU) 2015/478 as regards imports of Ukrainian products into the European Union
Subjects: Export/import control, trade defence, trade barriers Union Customs Code, tariffs, preferential arrangements, rules of origin
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0056(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to temporarily suspend certain import rules for Ukrainian products, originally outlined in Regulation (EU) 2015/478. This suspension, rooted in the EU-Ukraine Association Agreement, aims to support Ukraine's economy, particularly its iron and steel sectors, which have been severely impacted by the conflict with Russia. The regulation allows Ukrainian products to bypass specific EU surveillance and safeguard measures, facilitating easier market access. The European Commission is granted temporary authority to implement these suspensions for specific products, ensuring consistent application across the EU. This measure is set to last for three years, from June 6, 2025, to June 5, 2028, providing the Commission time to propose further legislative actions. The Commission can extend suspensions for individual products if their import levels threaten EU producers significantly. This regulation is directly applicable across all EU member states.
