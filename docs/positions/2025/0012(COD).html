Additional customs duties on imports of certain products originating in the USA
Subjects: Bilateral economic and trade agreements and relations Union Customs Code, tariffs, preferential arrangements, rules of origin
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0012(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have revised regulations on additional customs duties for certain U.S. imports, addressing issues related to the U.S. Continued Dumping and Subsidy Offset Act (CDSOA), which violated World Trade Organization rules. Originally, a 4.3% duty was imposed on specific U.S. products, but due to the minimal economic impact of the CDSOA—reduced to just USD 34.98 in 2024—the duty has been lowered to 0%. A "de minimis" threshold of USD 30,000 is now set, meaning no duty adjustments are needed if disbursements under the CDSOA fall below this amount, streamlining administrative processes. To ensure timely implementation, the regulation will take effect the day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union, bypassing the usual eight-week waiting period. These changes aim to align with international trade rules and efficiently manage the EU's economic interests.
