Modification of customs duties applicable to imports of certain goods originating in or exported directly or indirectly from Russia and Belarus
Subjects: Bilateral economic and trade agreements and relations Union Customs Code, tariffs, preferential arrangements, rules of origin
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0021(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to adjust customs duties on imports from Russia and Belarus, aiming to reduce the EU's reliance on these countries, particularly concerning urea and nitrogen-based fertilizers. This measure seeks to protect the EU's agricultural market and ensure food security by preventing market disruptions and supporting the EU's fertilizer industry amid high gas prices. The regulation imposes higher duties on goods from Belarus to avoid import diversion due to its ties with Russia and excludes these imports from reduced tariff rates. The EU can impose these duties under World Trade Organization (WTO) security exceptions, reflecting deteriorated relations with Russia and Belarus. The Commission will monitor fertilizer prices and import volumes, with the authority to impose additional duties if import thresholds are met. The regulation will take effect the day after its publication, with specific provisions applying from July 2025, and aims to prevent further economic dependence on these imports.
