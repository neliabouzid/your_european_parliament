Habitats Directive: protection status of the wolf (Canis lupus)
Subjects: Animal protection Protection of natural resources: fauna, flora, nature, wildlife, countryside; biodiversity
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0058(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have adopted an amendment to the Habitats Directive, altering the protection status of the wolf (Canis lupus) in Europe. This change follows a decision by the Bern Convention to reclassify the wolf from a strictly protected species to a protected species. Consequently, the Habitats Directive will be updated to reflect this new status, ensuring the wolf remains safeguarded under EU law. The amendment is designed to align EU conservation efforts with international obligations while allowing member states the flexibility to implement stricter protections if desired. The directive emphasizes the importance of achieving its objectives at the EU level, adhering to principles of subsidiarity and proportionality, ensuring actions are necessary and appropriate. Member states are required to implement the necessary legal measures by January 15, 2027, and must inform the Commission of these changes. This directive will become effective twenty days after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.
