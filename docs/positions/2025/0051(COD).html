Role of gas storage for securing gas supplies ahead of the winter season
Subjects: Gas, electricity, natural gas, biogas Security of energy supply Cooperation and agreements for energy
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0051(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have adopted a regulation to enhance gas supply security across the EU, requiring Member States to fill 90% of their gas storage by November 1st, with flexibility for deviations due to market or technical challenges. The regulation emphasizes EU-level coordination and a framework for managing gas supply until 2027, ensuring transparency and preventing market distortion. Member States without storage facilities must secure access to those in other states. They must demonstrate compliance and notify the Commission. Storage operators report filling levels to national authorities, who monitor and report to the Commission. The regulation includes measures for monitoring and aligning filling trajectories, with the Commission assisting Member States. It also addresses the use of Russian gas in storage. The regulation is binding and directly applicable across the EU, with specific provisions for shared responsibilities between Slovakia and the Czech Republic regarding the Dolní Bojanovice storage facility.
