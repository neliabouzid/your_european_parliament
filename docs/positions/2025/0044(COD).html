Certain corporate sustainability reporting and due diligence requirements: application dates
Subjects: Company law Sustainable development
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0044(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament has adopted a directive to amend corporate sustainability reporting and due diligence requirements, aligning with the European Green Deal and Sustainable Finance Action Plan. This directive extends application dates by two years to ease administrative burdens, with large companies and certain public-interest entities reporting in 2027 for financial years starting on or after January 1, 2026, and smaller companies in 2028 for financial years starting on or after January 1, 2027. Additionally, companies with significant employee numbers and turnover have specific deadlines for compliance, with some measures applying from July 2028 and others from July 2029. The directive aims to simplify the sustainability framework and reduce company burdens, emphasizing the EU's role in achieving these objectives at a union level. Member States must adopt necessary laws by December 31, 2025, and the directive will enter into force the day after its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.
