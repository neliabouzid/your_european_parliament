Amending Regulation as regards obligations of economic operators concerning battery due diligence policies
Subjects: Waste management, domestic waste, packaging, light industrial waste Dangerous substances, toxic and radioactive wastes (storage, transport) Sustainable development
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0129(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have amended Regulation (EU) 2023/1542, extending the due diligence requirements deadline for battery manufacturers dealing with cobalt, natural graphite, lithium, and nickel from August 18, 2025, to August 18, 2027. This extension aims to address challenges in raw material sourcing and third-party verification, allowing smoother adjustments in supply chains and the development of due diligence schemes. The amendment aligns with Directive (EU) 2024/1760, which mandates companies to address human rights and environmental impacts in their operations. To ensure consistency and legal certainty, the regulation will be published urgently in the Official Journal of the European Union, entering into force the following day. The European Union adopted these measures to better achieve the regulation's objectives at a Union level, emphasizing the efficient functioning of the internal market and the protection of human health and the environment.
