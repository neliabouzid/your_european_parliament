Additional assistance and further flexibility to outermost regions affected by severe natural disasters: cyclone Chido devastating Mayotte
Subjects: Natural disasters, Solidarity Fund Outlying and outermost regions, overseas countries and territories
Stage reached: Procedure completed
Reference: 2025/0104(COD)
Position Summary
The European Parliament and the Council have enacted measures to provide urgent support and flexibility to outermost regions like Mayotte, severely impacted by Cyclone Chido. The legislation, which amends existing regulations, aims to enhance financial aid through the European Agricultural Fund for Rural Development (EAFRD) to bolster local agricultural production and address food system vulnerabilities during recovery. It establishes a legal framework allowing affected regions to continue receiving support during restoration periods, emphasizing Union-level intervention. The regulation, emphasizing urgency, will take effect immediately upon publication in the Official Journal of the European Union. It allows for amendments to the POSEI programme, enabling continued support for agricultural activities despite reduced production capacity due to disasters. Additionally, Mayotte is permitted to approve support applications beyond the usual deadline and maintain EAFRD contributions to its rural development programme for the years 2021-2022. This approach underscores the commitment to mitigating the impact of natural disasters on vulnerable regions.
